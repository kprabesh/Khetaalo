Khtetaalo

is a non-profit organization which assists local farmers in various locations throughout Nepal by inviting individuals from all over the world to experience and help with Nepali farming traditions. Interns and volunteers get an opportunity to immerse and explore Nepali Farming Practices, Nepali Customs and Traditions, Nepali Festivals and Rituals.

We also partner with local businesses to encourage the local economy. We design our programs on the basis of festivals regarding farming (i.e.sowing season, harvesting season, etc) so volunteers and interns can have rewarding experiences and understand traditions and their significance on the farming practices of Nepal.

Families Reached
Niranjan Aryal

Khetaalo was a rewarding experience for me. I was delighted to be living with a Nepali family. They taught me how to cook, we cooked together. They even taught how to sing and dance. Above all, I was shadowing my mentor Hamphe from whom I learnt a lot about Nepalese farming practices. Hamphe was a funny and oftentimes he would share his stories while we sat on the edge of the field for a short break under a shade of a huge banyan tree.

Yogi Aryal

I changed my location from Pokhara to Siraha and I am so thankful that I did. I got to experience a completely different part of Nepal. Although in such proximity, those places were so diverse. I got to learn a lot about Nepali culture, their dresses and their festivals. I participated in many of Tharu festivals in Siraha since my host family were Tharu.
